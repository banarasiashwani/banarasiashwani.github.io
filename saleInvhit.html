Accounting Entries (in Indian Rupees)

1. Customer Account (Accounts Receivable)

Debit: Customer Account (Accounts Receivable)

Why: The customer owes the business money, increasing the asset (receivable).

Credit: Sales Revenue Account

Why: Revenue is earned from the sale, increasing equity.

Example:

If you invoice a customer for ₹1,000:

Debit: Accounts Receivable (Customer)  ₹1,000  
Credit: Sales Revenue                ₹1,000

2. Cost of Goods Sold (COGS) & Inventory

Debit: Cost of Goods Sold (COGS)

Why: The cost of inventory sold is recognized as an expense.

Credit: Inventory Account

Why: Inventory is reduced as goods are sold.

Example:

If the COGS for the sold item is ₹600:

Debit: COGS           ₹600  
Credit: Inventory      ₹600

3. Tax Accounts (GST/VAT/Sales Tax)

Debit: Accounts Receivable (Customer)

Why: The customer is charged tax, increasing the receivable.

Credit: Tax Payable Account (e.g., GST Payable)

Why: The business owes the tax amount to the government (liability).

Example:

If the invoice includes 10% GST (₹100):

Debit: Accounts Receivable  ₹100  
Credit: GST Payable         ₹100

4. Additional Charges

a) Freight Charges

Debit: Freight Expense Account

Credit: Accounts Payable

b) Cash Discounts

Debit: Sales Discounts Account

Credit: Accounts Receivable

Example:

If a ₹20 discount is offered:

Debit: Sales Discounts      ₹20  
Credit: Accounts Receivable ₹20

Full Example of Posted Sales Invoice

Invoice Details:

  • Sales Amount: ₹1,000
  • GST: ₹100 (10%)
  • COGS: ₹600

Entries:

1. Debit: Accounts Receivable       ₹1,100 (Sales + Tax)  
   Credit: Sales Revenue            ₹1,000  
   Credit: GST Payable              ₹100  

2. Debit: COGS                      ₹600  
   Credit: Inventory                ₹600
