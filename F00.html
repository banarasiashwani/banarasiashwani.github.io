Financial Concepts

1. Assets

Assets are resources owned by a business that have economic value and are expected to provide future benefits. They are classified as either Current Assets (short-term) or Non-Current Assets (long-term).

Examples of Assets:

  • Current Assets:
    • Cash (e.g., RBL Bank, AXIS Bank)
    • Accounts Receivable (e.g., amounts owed by customers)
    • Inventory (e.g., goods for sale)
    • Prepaid Expenses (e.g., Prepaid Rent, Prepaid Insurance)
  • Non-Current Assets:
    • Fixed Assets (e.g., Equipment, Buildings)
    • Accumulated Depreciation (e.g., reduction in value of fixed assets)
    • Intangible Assets (e.g., patents, trademarks)

How Assets Work:

  • Assets are recorded on the Balance Sheet.
  • They have a debit balance (positive value).

2. Liabilities

Liabilities are obligations or debts that a business owes to external parties. They are classified as either Current Liabilities (short-term) or Non-Current Liabilities (long-term).

Examples of Liabilities:

  • Current Liabilities:
    • Accounts Payable (e.g., amounts owed to suppliers)
    • Sales Tax Payable (e.g., taxes collected from customers)
    • Accrued Expenses (e.g., Accrued Salaries & Wages)
    • Short-Term Loans
  • Non-Current Liabilities:
    • Long-Term Loans (e.g., Notes Payable)
    • Deferred Tax Liabilities

How Liabilities Work:

  • Liabilities are recorded on the Balance Sheet.
  • They have a credit balance (negative value).

3. Equity

Equity represents the owner's claim on the assets of the business after all liabilities have been paid. It is also known as Shareholder's Equity or Owner's Equity.

Examples of Equity:

  • Capital Stock (e.g., funds raised by issuing shares)
  • Retained Earnings (e.g., profits reinvested in the business)
  • Dividends (e.g., distributions to shareholders)

How Equity Works:

  • Equity is recorded on the Balance Sheet.
  • It has a credit balance (negative value).

4. Revenue

Revenue represents the income earned by a business from its primary operations, such as selling goods or providing services. It is also called Income or Sales.

Examples of Revenue:

  • Income from Services (e.g., consulting fees)
  • Product Sales (e.g., sales of goods)
  • Interest Income (e.g., earnings from investments)
  • Job Sales (e.g., revenue from completed projects)

How Revenue Works:

  • Revenue is recorded on the Income Statement.
  • It has a credit balance (negative value).

5. Expenses

Expenses represent the costs incurred by a business to generate revenue. They are deducted from revenue to calculate net income.

Examples of Expenses:

  • Cost of Goods Sold (e.g., Cost of Materials, Cost of Labour)
  • Operating Expenses (e.g., Rent Expense, Advertising Expense)
  • Payroll Expenses (e.g., Salaries Expense, Payroll Tax Expense)
  • Depreciation Expense (e.g., reduction in value of fixed assets)
  • Interest Expense (e.g., cost of borrowing money)

How Expenses Work:

  • Expenses are recorded on the Income Statement.
  • They have a debit balance (positive value).

Summary of Account Types

Category Description Balance Type Financial Statement
Assets Resources owned by the business Debit (+) Balance Sheet
Liabilities Obligations owed by the business Credit (-) Balance Sheet
Equity Owner's claim on the business Credit (-) Balance Sheet
Revenue Income earned from operations Credit (-) Income Statement
Expenses Costs incurred to generate revenue Debit (+) Income Statement

How These Categories Work Together

Balance Sheet:

Assets = Liabilities + Equity

This equation must always balance.

Income Statement:

Net Income = Revenue - Expenses

Net Income flows into the Equity section of the Balance Sheet.
